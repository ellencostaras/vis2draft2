Global Trade of Human Hair

Data obtained from the UN Comtrade Database.

Today, hair is more than just material, its big business. Millions of dollars are made trading this commodity. By using the slider above, the choropleth map displays the countries who export human hair. Over time, the big players have established themselves: USA, China, Tanzania, Pakistan, Myanmar, and most notably, India.

Where does all the hair go?

The map to the left displays hair export data for 2022. Each bubble on the map is the exporter, and by hovering over them, we can see all of the countries it exports to. Clearly, this is an incredibly globalised industry. However, like most globalised industries, the hair trade is incredibly exploitative. Much of the hair comes from small sellers who tour villages in developing countires, offering poverty-stricken women a small payment to part with their hair. This is communicated further in the heat map and area charts below.

The heat map above only shows data for those countries which exported hair every year from 2011 to 2022, and is sorted from largest to smallest exporter. Still, the trends are clear: The largest exporters are those with the cheapest prices, such as India. A notable exception is the USA, who still have cheap prices despite lower exports. This may be because the US is where businesses are based, acting as a third party in the supply chain.
For other countries such Myanmar however, we only see a breif peak in their exports, shown by the lefthand area charts above. In Myanmar, donating hair became increasingly popular for religious reasons in 2017. This lead to Myanmar diving head first into the human hair trade, so much so that demand became greater than supply! The effects of this can be seen below in the streamgraph. It shows how Myanmar was forced to import millions of kilograms of hair, to mix in with their own supply to keep up with demand.
